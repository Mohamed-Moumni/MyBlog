Summary

Same as Ever by Morgan Housel explores timeless principles of human behavior, risk, expectations, and storytelling that remain constant despite changing times. The book examines fundamental truths about how people think, make decisions, and navigate uncertainty.

Key Takeaways

  • Hanging by a Thread - If you know where we have been, you realize we have no idea where we’re going.

  • Risk is What You Don’t See - Risk is what’s left over after you think you’ve thought of everything. Risk is all the things that came surprisingly into your life without alerting you.

  • Expectations and Reality - The world isn’t driven by greed, it’s driven by envy. The first law of happiness is low expectations. The expectation side of the equation is often more in your control than managing your circumstances.

  • Wild Minds - People who think about the world in unique ways you like also think about the world in unique ways you won’t like. If you’re not willing to do a wholesale swap with who that person is, then there is no point in being jealous.

  • Wild Numbers - People don’t want accuracy, they want certainty.

  • Best Story Wins - Humor is the best way to show you’re smart without bragging. When topics are complex, stories are like leverage. The most persuasive stories are about what you want to believe is true.

My Thoughts

This book provides valuable insights into timeless human behaviors and decision-making patterns that remain constant across different eras and contexts.
